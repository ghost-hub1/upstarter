What is your credit score?

Credit score ranges between 300 and 850.

0
Learn about credit score

A credit score aims to take all of the relevant information in your credit report and condense it into just a 3-digit number. At Upstart, our AI model looks at more than your credit score by factoring non-traditional variables, such as education and employment, because credit scores dont do a perfect job of predicting credit risk in all situations.

But the reality is that credit scores are widely used by traditional institutions as an easy way to assess creditworthiness. A credit score is rated on a scale ranging from 300 to 850 and can impact almost every aspect of your lifefrom a given interest rate on a loan to being selected for that apartment youve been looking at. So, its important to understand your score, the contributing factors, and ways to optimize.

Learn more about different tiers in the credit score range

Excellent credit 800 - 850

Excellent credit tells lenders that you have a very low probability of defaulting on a loan obligation. Credit scores of 800 or above are generally considered to be exceptional, and an 850 credit score is considered to be perfect credit.

Scores in this range are well above the average of U.S. consumers. Scores in this range mean that you generally pay your bills on time and have a history of responsible credit usage. Credit Score FAQs

There are several companies that provide credit scoring models. The FICO credit score, which is created and implemented by the Fair Isaac Corporation, is by far the most widely used. Another popular model is the VantageScore credit scoring model, which is jointly owned by the three major credit bureausExperian, Equifax, and TransUnion.

There are other credit scoring models that certain lenders may use. However, these are the largest, and the vast majority of loan decisions use either the FICO score or the VantageScore model.

Each model can produce several different scores. For example, you have different FICO Scores for your credit reports from each major credit bureau. Plus, there are versions specifically intended for things like auto lending and credit cards.

While the popular credit scoring models all use the same general information from your credit report, there are a couple of important things to know. First, the actual formulas used to calculate your credit score are a well-guarded secret. And second, the FICO score is by far the most popular among lenders, so well take a closer look at how it works.

The FICO credit scoring formula takes five different categories of information into account. While we dont know the exact methodology, we do know the relative weights of each category:

  1. Payment history (35% of your score)
    The most important component of your credit score is your payment history. In other words, paying your bills on time every month is the best way to build your credit score over time.
  2. Amounts owed (30%)
    The second most important component of your credit score is the amounts you owe. And the most important factors are your credit account balances relative to your credit limits or original loan amounts.
  3. Length of credit history (15%)
    In general, the longer your credit history, the better. The formula considers time-related data, such as the time since your oldest account was opened, the average age of your open credit accounts, and the ages of your individual accounts.
  4. New credit (10%)
    There are two specific types of information in this categoryinquiries and new accounts. The latter is self-explanatory. A credit inquiry happens when you apply for new credit, and a hard credit inquiry is completed.
  5. Credit mix (10%)
    Creditors want to know that you can be responsible with different types of creditnot just one or two. For example, if you have a mortgage, auto loan, and credit card, it could be counted more favorably than if you just had one of the three.

Theres no set-in-stone definition of what a good credit score is, or what scores fall into the realm of bad credit, but here are some loose guidelines. FICO has a credit score range of 300 to 850, and the following is a general framework of the various credit tiers.

  • Excellent credit: 800 or higher
  • Very Good: 740-799
  • Good: 670-739
  • Fair: 580-669
  • Poor: Below 580

If your credit score isnt exactly where you want it to be, its a worthwhile goal to try and improve your credit score. The good news is that it changes as soon as new information is added to (or removed from) your credit report. With the knowledge of how your credit score works and a little strategy, you might be surprised at how much of an impact you can have.

As a final note, if you arent sure where you stand, there are several ways to check your credit score. You may be able to get a free credit score (FICO score or VantageScore) through one of your credit cards, as many offer this as a cardholder perk. Or, if you want to see all of the different versions of your FICO score, you can buy them directly from Fair Isaac at myFICO.com.
